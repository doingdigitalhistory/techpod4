Introduction

Argument: The primary sources of Mark Twain show his levels of dislike towards hypocrisy, which he demonstrates in his writings. In The Adventures of Tom Sawyer, he shows how adults value virtue but reward misbehavior. Across his works, Twain critiques law, religion, and society for moral contradictions.

About Mark Twain

Mark Twain (1835–1910) was an American writer and humorist known for his sharp wit and social critique. His works often expose hypocrisy in social norms, politics, and morality.
