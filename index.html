Introduction

Argument: Mark Twain’s works reveal his deep dislike for hypocrisy. Through satire and sharp critique, Twain exposes contradictions in morality, law, and social norms. In The Adventures of Tom Sawyer, adults preach virtue but reward misbehavior—a theme echoed across his writings.

About Mark Twain

Mark Twain (1835–1910) was an American writer, humorist, and social critic. His works challenge readers to question authority and confront moral contradictions. Twain’s wit and insight make him one of the most influential voices in American literature.
