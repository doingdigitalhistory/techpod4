Adventures of Huckleberry Finn (1884)

Hypocrisy critiqued: Society claims to be “civilized” while upholding slavery. Finn’s moral dilemma of helping Jim shows the contradiction between social norms and morality. Twain critiques law, religion, and “respectable” society for living with injustice.

The Adventures of Tom Sawyer (1876)

Hypocrisy critiqued: Adults enforce rules they themselves break, while children act more honestly. Twain critiques conformity and adherence to small-town norms, showing how “respectable” people often behave worse than mischievous children.

To the Person Sitting in the Darkness (1901)

Hypocrisy critiqued: Twain attacks Western imperialism and missionary work, mocking the idea of “civilizing” foreign people while committing violence and theft. He critiques American and European moral contradictions in global politics.

The United States of Lyncherdom (1901)

Hypocrisy critiqued: Points out the contradiction of a nation that claims freedom and justice while tolerating lynching and mob violence. Shows Twain’s shift from satire to blunt moral outrage.
