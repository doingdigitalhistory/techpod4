Mark Twain consistently exposed hypocrisy in morality, law, and social norms. His works remain relevant today as societies continue to grapple with contradictions between ideals and actions. Twain’s critique challenges readers to question authority and embrace genuine morality.

Why It Matters Today

Hypocrisy still shapes modern politics, social norms, and global relations. Twain’s insights remind us to look beyond appearances and demand integrity in both personal and public life.
